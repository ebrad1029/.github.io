[ TWITTER SENTIMENT ANALYSIS FOR STATE FARM’S CRITICAL EVENTS ]
With the recent rise in feedback shared on social media platforms, companies have been collecting data
to get a better understanding on how their customers feel towards their company and their products.
Sentiment Analysis is an automated process where machine learning techniques are applied to identify or extract the subjective information of a text.
It is used to provide companies with interesting insights about their customers’ feedback and
therefore, sentiment analysis is proven essential for any company that wants to know how their company is doing.
In this project, we want to grasp the overall perception of State Farm’s customers through sentiment analysis among the social media platforms that State Farm is associated with.
We will do this by collecting customers’ tweets that were posted during critical State Farm events (i.e. The Superbowl)
and then, with that data, we will help find how customers’ feel towards State Farm.
This data will be put into graphs created in R and Python and then presented to State Farm in the form of a website.
Our goal for this project is to be able to present to State Farm with a sentiment analysis of the general sentiment towards their company over time.
Our final analysis would include the peaks and valleys in sentiment during critical events in the company’s history (new commercial, natural disaster, scandals, PR moves, etc.).
We would present this data to State Farm with various graphs posted on a website just for them.
The types of graphs would vary and will be based off of different types of data that we gather from twitter.
State Farm can then use this data to make their company the best that it can be.
This data will not only help State Farm, but it will help their customers as well, as State Farm will be able to use these results
to help better their customers either through providing better options for their products or by providing better customer service.
